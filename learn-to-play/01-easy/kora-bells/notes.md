# Kora Bells

**Provenance:** Original exercise.

**Purpose:** This exercise isolates the highest strings of each bridge row
(F5/C5 left-bridge; G5/D5 right-bridge) to develop consistent tone in the
upper register where the kora's bell-like quality is most prominent.

**How to play:**
- Bars 1–4: play the high strings of the left bridge (f, c) then right bridge
  (g, d) alternating.
- Bars 5–8: mix the two bridges in a single phrase, practising the hand switch.

**Practice tips:**
- Higher strings require less pluck force — the shorter string length makes
  them more sensitive. Keep your snap light and even.
- Let every note decay fully before the next pluck. If you hear muddiness,
  you're plucking before the previous string has rung out.
