# Kaira Pattern

**Provenance:** "Kaira" is one of the foundational pieces of the Mande griot
repertoire, associated with peace and calm. The melodic pattern here is drawn
from the traditional kumbengo (ostinato) basis. Traditional/folk — public domain.

**Range:** F3–F5.

**Performance context:** In the griot tradition, Kaira is played at celebrations,
naming ceremonies, and during moments of praise. The repetitive nature is
intentional — the kumbengo ostinato creates a hypnotic groove that supports
improvisation and song.

**Practice tips:**
- Play this slowly at first (♩=60) and let each string ring fully before plucking
  the next.
- The G (right bridge) in bar 2 and bar 6 creates a slight harmonic tension
  against the F-centre — that's the jazz-like colour of the kora.
- Once you can loop the pattern smoothly, try adding a simple thumb drone on F3
  every 4 beats.
