# Tutu Jara Motif

**Provenance:** Drawn from the traditional Mande piece "Tutu Jara" (Lion of
Tutu), a classic griot composition. This is a simplified motif from the
kumbengo line. Traditional/folk — public domain.

**Range:** F3–F5. 12/8 compound feel — think "ONE-and-a, TWO-and-a."

**Why this is intermediate:**
- 12/8 compound meter requires internalising a triplet subdivision while
  maintaining a steady pulse.
- The interlock between the F-pentatonic left bridge and the G/D right bridge
  becomes more complex here — you need to switch hands mid-phrase.

**Performance notes:**
- In the griot tradition, this piece is often played in praise of a noble
  hunter. The 12/8 feel gives it a swaying, processional quality.
- The long held tones (F9 = dotted quarter note × 3) should resonate fully
  before the next phrase begins — use this space to re-position your hands.
